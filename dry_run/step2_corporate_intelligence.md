### Entity 1 — Andrew Fastow (CFO, Enron Corp)

**Registration / role:** Chief Financial Officer of Enron Corp 1998–2001. Named general partner and manager of LJM Cayman L.P. and LJM2 Co-Investment L.P. — both off-balance-sheet vehicles he created while simultaneously serving as Enron's CFO, a clear conflict of interest.

**Enforcement actions:**
- **October 2002:** DOJ indicted Fastow on 78 counts of fraud, money laundering, and conspiracy
- **January 2004:** Pleaded guilty to two counts of conspiracy (wire and securities fraud); agreed to cooperate with the broader Enron investigation
- **September 2006:** Sentenced to 6 years in federal prison; forfeited $23.8M
- **SEC civil action:** Permanently barred from serving as officer or director of a public company; ordered to disgorge $23.8M (SEC LR-18543)
- Released to a halfway house May 2011; full release December 2011

**LJM structure:** LJM Cayman was incorporated in the Cayman Islands. Fastow named it after his wife Lea and sons Jeffrey and Matthew (L-J-M). The entity was used to absorb Enron's poorly performing assets off the balance sheet while reporting false profits.

**Sources:**

- [DOJ: Fastow Guilty Plea](https://www.justice.gov/archive/opa/pr/2004/January/04_crm_019.htm)
- [SEC Litigation Release LR-18543](https://www.sec.gov/enforcement-litigation/litigation-releases/lr-18543)

### Entity 2 — Chewco Investments L.P. / Michael Kopper

**Structure:** Chewco was a limited partnership formed in 1997, managed by Michael Kopper (Enron managing director) under Fastow's hidden control. Named after the Star Wars character Chewbacca (matching the JEDI theme). Purpose: to buy CalPERS' stake in JEDI, keeping JEDI off Enron's balance sheet.

**The fraud mechanism:** Chewco failed the 3% third-party equity rule for off-balance-sheet treatment. As a result, Enron should have consolidated Chewco and JEDI — adding approximately $600M in hidden debt and removing ~$400M in nominal profits from its reported financials.

**Illegal payments:**
- Dec 1997–Dec 2000: Kopper received $1.5M in undisclosed management fees, shared with Fastow
- March 2001: Enron bought Chewco's JEDI interest for $35M — Kopper and partner pocketed ~$3M
- December 1998: $400K "nuisance fee" paid, of which Kopper transferred $67K to Fastow

**Enforcement actions:**
- **August 2002:** Kopper became first Enron executive to plead guilty (money laundering, wire fraud)
- **SEC action LR-17692:** Permanently barred from public company officer/director roles; ordered to disgorge and forfeit approximately $12M
- Kopper sentenced to 37 months in prison

**Sources:**
- [SEC LR-17692: Michael J. Kopper](https://www.sec.gov/enforcement-litigation/litigation-releases/lr-17692)
- [Wikipedia: Chewco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chewco)
- [DOJ Kopper charging document](https://www.justice.gov/archive/dag/cftf/chargingdocs/kopperinformation.pdf)

### Entity 3 — JEDI Investments L.P. (Joint Energy Development)

**Structure:** JEDI was a $500M joint venture formed in 1993 between Enron and CalPERS. When CalPERS wanted to exit in 1997, Enron created Chewco to buy them out — rather than consolidating JEDI onto its balance sheet. JEDI was used as a vehicle to funnel money back to Enron (the round-trip pattern in Step 1).

**CalPERS connection:** CalPERS initially invested $250M. After the Chewco buyout, JEDI became wholly Enron-controlled but was still treated as an independent entity for accounting purposes — enabling round-trip transactions and inflated earnings.

**Sources:**
- [JEDI and Chewco case study](https://enron.net/jedi-and-chewco-a-case-study)
- [SEC LR-17762 (Fastow civil action)](https://www.sec.gov/enforcement-litigation/litigation-releases/lr-17762)

### Entity 4 — LJM Cayman L.P.

**Jurisdiction:** Cayman Islands (high-secrecy jurisdiction, no public beneficial ownership registry)
**Incorporation:** 1999, created by Fastow while serving as Enron CFO
**Purpose:** To purchase poorly performing Enron assets at inflated values, enabling Enron to recognise gains and hide losses. Fastow earned over $45M in undisclosed fees from LJM entities.

**ICIJ Offshore Leaks:** The name "LJM" appears in the ICIJ database across multiple jurisdictions. While not all matches are the specific Enron entity, the Cayman Islands incorporation is consistent with its known structure.

**Board approval conflict:** Enron's board twice waived its conflict-of-interest rules to allow Fastow to manage LJM while serving as CFO — a governance failure central to the scandal.

**Sources:**
- [Enron Scandal — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enron_scandal)
- [SEC Fastow civil action](https://www.sec.gov/news/press/2004-6.htm)

---

### Entity 5 — Southampton Place L.P.

**Structure:** A limited partnership involving Fastow family members and Enron employees. The September 2001 $26.8M payment was the buyout of Southampton's stake — a transaction where Fastow, Kopper, and associates pocketed millions through undisclosed side deals.

**The Southampton buyout:** A group of Enron employees invested $25,000 in Southampton in March 2001 and received back approximately $4.5M in two months — a 180x return on investment, funded by Enron shareholders.

---

### Network Centre: Andrew Fastow

The public record places **Andrew Fastow** at the centre of this network:
- Created LJM Cayman (offshore), JEDI (re-structure), and indirectly controlled Chewco
- Used Michael Kopper as a nominee to hide his involvement in Chewco
- Earned an estimated **$45M+ in undisclosed fees** from entities he simultaneously managed as Enron's CFO
- Convicted, sentenced to 6 years, forfeited $23.8M

**Second-tier actors:** Michael Kopper (Chewco manager), Ben Glisan (Enron treasurer, approved accounting treatment), Richard Causey (Chief Accounting Officer), Jeff Skilling (aware of off-balance-sheet strategy per his own emails).

## Quality Assessment

**Did web search return useful results?** Yes — DOJ, SEC, Wikipedia, and court documents are all indexed and return clean results. The key enforcement actions (DOJ press releases, SEC litigation releases) are all publicly accessible.

**Confidence level of findings:** High — these are confirmed criminal convictions and SEC enforcement actions on the public record.

**Data freshness note:** Company registration data in the Cayman Islands is not publicly searchable. The ICIJ Offshore Leaks database provides the best public evidence of offshore structures.
